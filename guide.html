Floods: Do’s & Don’ts

Do's:

  • Avoid building in flood prone areas unless you elevate and reinforce your home.
  • Contact community officials to find out if they are planning to construct barriers (levees, beams and floodwalls) to stop floodwater from entering the homes in your area.
  • Seal the walls in your basement with waterproofing compounds to avoid seepage.
  • Listen to the radio or television for information.
  • Be aware that flash flooding can occur. If there is any possibility of a flash flood, move immediately to higher ground. Do not wait for instructions to move.
  • Be aware of streams, drainage channels, canyons, and other areas known to flood suddenly. Flash floods can occur in these areas with or without such typical warnings as rain clouds or heavy rain.
  • Secure your home. If you have time, bring in outdoor furniture. Move essential items to an upper floor.
  • Turn off utilities at the main switches or valves if instructed to do so. Disconnect electrical appliances. Do not touch electrical equipment if you are wet or standing in water.

Don'ts:

  • Do not walk through moving water. Six inches of moving water can make you fall. If you have to walk in water, walk where the water is not moving. Use a stick to check the firmness of the ground in front of you.
  • Do not drive into flooded areas. If floodwaters rise around your car, abandon the car and move to higher ground if you can do so safely. You and the vehicle can be quickly swept away.

Earthquake Safety Guide: Do’s & Don’ts

  • Repair deep plaster cracks and fix structural defects.
  • Anchor overhead lighting and secure shelves to walls.
  • Identify safe places indoors (under sturdy furniture, against inner walls) and outdoors (open areas away from buildings and utilities).
  • Prepare a disaster kit with essentials: torch, radio, first aid, food, water, medicines, and emergency tools.
  • Develop an emergency communication plan with a family contact.
  • Engage the community in earthquake preparedness: educate, publish emergency info, and conduct drills.
  • Drop, cover, and hold on under sturdy furniture or in a safe spot.
  • Stay away from glass, windows, and outside doors.
  • Remain in bed if safe, protecting your head with a pillow.
  • Stay indoors until the shaking stops and it’s safe to exit.
  • Move away from buildings, trees, and utility wires.
  • Stay in an open area until the shaking stops.

    • If in a Vehicle:

    • Stop safely and stay inside. Avoid stopping near buildings or overpasses.
    • Proceed cautiously after the shaking stops, avoiding damaged roads.

    If Trapped Under Debris:

    • Do not light a match or kick up dust.
    • Cover your mouth, tap on a pipe or wall to signal rescuers, and use a whistle if available. Shout only as a last resort.

    Landslide Safety: Do’s and Don’ts

    Do's:

    • Stay calm and alert.
    • Follow evacuation orders and instructions from authorities.
    • Seek higher ground or move to elevated locations.
    • Move indoors to upper floors, away from doors and windows.
    • Avoid steep slopes, cliffs, and hillsides.
    • Monitor for signs of ground movement.
    • Stay informed through local news and emergency broadcasts.

    Don’ts:

    • Don’t panic or make hasty decisions.
    • Don’t ignore evacuation orders or warnings.
    • Don’t stay near or drive through high-risk areas.
    • Don’t remain near slopes, rivers, or streams.
    • Don’t stay near windows or doors during a landslide.
    • Don’t approach or touch unstable structures or debris.
    • Don’t rely on rumours or unverified information.

    Wildfire Safety: Do’s and Don’ts

    Do's:

    • Stay informed with local news and alerts about wildfire conditions.
    • Follow evacuation orders immediately when given.
    • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like water, food, and important documents.
    • Wear protective clothing (long sleeves, pants, and masks) to reduce exposure to smoke and embers.
    • Keep doors and windows shut to prevent smoke from entering your home.
    • Remove flammable materials from around your home and create a defensible space.
    • Turn on lights to increase visibility in smoke-heavy areas.

    Don’ts:

    • Don’t ignore evacuation orders or delay leaving.
    • Don’t leave doors or windows open, which can allow embers to enter your home.
    • Don’t drive through heavy smoke or wildfire zones if you can avoid it.
    • Don’t use flammable items like gas-powered equipment during high fire risk periods.
    • Don’t stay near areas with dense vegetation or dry brush.
    • Don’t rely solely on automated systems for evacuation alerts—stay alert and proactive.
